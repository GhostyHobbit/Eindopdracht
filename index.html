Our Solar System

Our solar system exists out of nine planets, eight for the people who hate Pluto. It's also home to a total of 205 moons of which Saturn houses the most of them, with 82 moons. The order of the planets from closes to the sun towards the outer rings is: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Neptune, Uranus and Pluto. The planets outside of the astroid belt are all gass giants, besides Pluto which is actually a glorified chunk of rock about as big as our moon. There are a few more glorified rocks like Ceres, another dwarf planet, Chiron, a rock somewhere between an astroid and a planetoid, and Lilith, a metallic astroid in the astroid belt.

Space Bodies

Mercury

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Mercury is about as two-faced as I am. It's surface is super hot during day time and icy cold during the night. A day on Mercury is also twice as long as a year on Mercury. This means that every day you have two birthdays. Astrologically Mercury is the planet of communication and travels.

Moon

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

Our Moon is a big chonky rock that is fully illuminated every 28 days. It's the only space body that has ever been touched by human feet. It has not yet been proven if there is actually a man on the moon that creeps at us. Astrologically the Moon stands for emotions and mental processes.

Pluto

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

Pluto is officially a dwarf planet, no bigger than our Moon. A day on Pluto lasts as long as 6.4 Earth days. One year on Pluto lasts almost 250 Earth years. Astrologically Pluto is the planet of transformation and destruction.
